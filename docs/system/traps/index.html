Traps

Edit on GitHub

Overview

Traps are found on the ground and activate when stepped on,
or when an object is thrown or dropped on top of them.

Allies who are thrown onto traps receive the same effect as Shiren stepping on them,
but ally monsters receive the same effect of an enemy monster stepping on them.

Example: Asuka's current HP is halved when she is thrown onto a Landmine,
but an ally Mamel that is thrown onto a Landmine instantly collapses.

Traps can be destroyed by splashing water on them from a Water Pot.
However, traps can't be destroyed by explosions or the effect of a Flood Trap.
Traps thrown into water will vanish with the message "[Trap name] disappeared..."
Piko Hammers can destroy visible traps, and a Knockback Staff can be used to send
a trap flying, dealing a small amount of damage to a target and making the trap vanish.

Hidden traps can't be detected by the [Ground] menu option.
Swinging a Mallet over a tile with a hidden trap will reveal the trap without destroying it,
and swinging the Mallet again over the visible trap will destroy the trap.
Hidden traps can be destroyed in one turn using a Mallet if Shiren ate Sight Grass.

Damage dealt by traps increases as Shiren levels up when he has Trapper status,
but Shiren's level is not a factor for damage dealt when he doesn't have Trapper status.

Experience Points

Shiren with Trapper status gains exp equal to 95% of the normal amount when he defeats
an enemy using a trap, or 75% of the normal amount if the trap doesn't defeat the enemy.
However, Spoil Trap will be the same amount as not defeating an enemy using a trap.
Shiren only gains 20% of the normal amount if he defeats enemies using direct attacks.

Chain Trap Experience Points

Chain Traps activate all other traps in a 1-tile radius, potentially granting lots of exp.
The chain can be continued by placing traps at the end points of One-Way and Log traps,
or by placing Landmines in close proximity so that they explode one after the other.
The formula used in calculating experience points in a trap chain is shown below.

n = Number of traps that trapped an enemy prior to the Chain Trap activating.
m = Numer of Chain Traps that triggered due to a trap that moves an enemy (max: 1),
or the number of Landmines that exploded away from a Chain Trap.
N = Number of traps that trapped an enemy due to a Chain Trap activating.
M = Number of traps activated by a Chain Trap that didn't trap an enemy.
L = Number of Landmines that exploded within a 1-tile radius of a Chain Trap.
(L isn't included in M, and the number of Chain Traps isn't counted for n or N)

Exp = Normal amount * 0.75 *
[n^2 + n * m * (2 * N + 1) + 0.5 * N * (N + 3) + M * (n + 1) + L * (n + N / 2)]

However, a Chain Trap on its own: Exp = Normal amount * (3 / 4)

If the trap defeated an enemy, the following is added to the amount of exp:

Normal amount * (1 / 5) * (n + N)
